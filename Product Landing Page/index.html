The flute is a member of a family of musical instruments in the woodwind group. Like all woodwinds, flutes are aerophones, producing sound with a vibrating column of air. Unlike woodwind instruments with reeds, a flute produces sound when the player's air flows across an opening. A musician who plays the flute is called a flautist or flutist.

The price of a flute in Japan can vary depending on the type of flute and its quality. Traditional Japanese flutes such as the shakuhachi and fue can cost anywhere from ¥10,000 to ¥100,000 (approximately $91 to $910 USD).
